STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)

ACRYLIC ACID LEAK EMERGENCY RESPONSE

CHEMICAL HAZARD INFORMATION: ACRYLIC ACID

CAS Number: 79-10-7

Hazards: Corrosive, flammable liquid, toxic by inhalation, causes severe skin burns and eye damage

Exposure Limits: TWA: 2 ppm (ACGIH), STEL: 15 ppm

Flash Point: 50°C (122°F)

Auto-ignition Temperature: 438°C (820°F)

LEAK DETECTION
• Visual observation
• Acrid odor detection
• Leak detection alarms
• Process monitoring anomalies
RAISE ALARM
• Activate emergency alarm
• Notify shift supervisor
• Contact emergency response team
• Call control room: [EMERGENCY NUMBER]
Assess Leak Severity
MINOR LEAK
IMMEDIATE RESPONSE
• Don appropriate PPE
• Isolate area (10m radius)
• Stop leak at source if safe
• Use acid spill kit
MAJOR LEAK
EVACUATION & RESPONSE
• Evacuate area (100m radius)
• Activate plant emergency response
• Notify external emergency services
• Alert nearby facilities
CONTROL MEASURES
• Establish hot/warm/cold zones
• Deploy vapor suppression (foam/water spray)
• Contain runoff with dikes/absorbents
• Monitor air quality (LEL, VOC readings)
CLEANUP PROCEDURES
• Neutralize with sodium carbonate/bicarbonate
• Absorb with inert material
• Collect in appropriate containers
• Dispose as hazardous waste
• Decontaminate area with alkaline solution
POST-INCIDENT ACTIONS
• Medical assessment of exposed personnel
• Document incident details
• Conduct investigation
• Report to regulatory authorities
• Implement corrective actions

REQUIRED PPE FOR ACRYLIC ACID RESPONSE:

  • Respiratory Protection: SCBA or full-face respirator with organic vapor/acid gas cartridge
  • Skin Protection: Chemical-resistant suit (Level A/B for major leaks)
  • Hand Protection: Butyl rubber or neoprene gloves
  • Eye Protection: Chemical splash goggles and face shield
  • Foot Protection: Chemical-resistant boots

CRITICAL SAFETY NOTES:

  • Acrylic acid can polymerize explosively if exposed to heat or incompatible materials
  • Always approach from upwind direction
  • Remove all ignition sources
  • Do not use water jet on leaking material
  • Prevent entry into waterways, sewers, or confined areas
